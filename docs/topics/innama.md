# Innamā (إِنَّمَا) — The Restriction Particle

**Summary**: إِنَّمَا restricts the predicate exclusively to the subject stated — everything else is implicitly negated. Grammatically, the مَا cancels إِنَّ's effect, leaving the noun after it in its default marfūʿ state.

---

## Structure

**إِنَّمَا** = **إِنَّ** + **مَا** (the kāffah — the blocking مَا)

Normally, **إِنَّ** pulls the following noun into naṣb (accusative). But when **مَا** is suffixed, it **blocks (kaffat) the grammatical effect of إِنَّ**, so the noun remains **marfūʿ** (nominative):

| Form | Effect on following noun |
|---|---|
| **إِنَّ الرَّجُلَ** كريم | naṣb — الرَّجُلَ is manṣūb |
| **إِنَّمَا الرَّجُلُ** كريم | marfūʿ — الرَّجُلُ is marfūʿ (مَا blocked إِنَّ's naṣb) |

---

## Meaning: Exclusive Restriction

**إِنَّمَا** asserts: *"Only and nothing but..."* — everything other than what is stated is negated or set aside.

!!! example "Surah Al-Hujuraat 49:15"
    **إِنَّمَا المُؤْمِنُونَ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا بِاللَّهِ وَرَسُولِهِ ثُمَّ لَمْ يَرْتَابُوا**

    *"The believers are **only** those who believe in Allah and His Messenger, then have no doubt..."*

    The use of إِنَّمَا restricts true belief to those who meet this full description — excluding those who merely claim it outwardly.

---

## Why مَا is Called الكافّة

_Kaffat_ (كَفَّتْ) means "it restrained/blocked." This مَا restrains إِنَّ from exercising its grammatical influence on the following noun. The term **حرف كافّ** (blocking particle) applies to مَا in several similar constructions.
